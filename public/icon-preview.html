Design Rationale

Play → Eye: The game's core mechanic is perception — seeing a color and remembering it. An eye with visible iris communicates "look carefully" more directly than an abstract shape. The inner dot represents the pupil/focus point.

Match → Overlapping swatches: Two rounded rectangles overlapping in the center — a Venn-diagram-like gesture that immediately reads as "compare two things side by side", which is exactly the mode's mechanic.

Picture It → Triadic wheel: Simplified from 6 spokes to 3 — reduces visual noise while preserving the color wheel concept. Three spokes align with the triadic color harmony (primary relationships), reinforcing the "identify the right color" mechanic.

Field picker → Crosshair with position dot: The filled dot at (15,9) shows where your current selection sits in the 2D space — making the icon itself a miniature of the picker interface.

History vs Replay: History has the clock hands (looking back at time). Replay is a pure circular arrow (doing it again) — the clock face distinguishes "review past" from "start fresh".
